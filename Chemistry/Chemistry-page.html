            Bohr atomic model
            The Bohr model is considered the predecessor of the quantum mechanics model and the first that incorporated quantum theory. The Bohr model and others describes the properties of atomic electrons of a set of allowed values. Atoms absorb or emit radiation only when the electrons abruptly jump between allowed, or stationary states.
            Before 1913 the atom was always thought of to have a tiny heavily positive charge called a nucleus. But bhor thought otherwise, what he thought of was by limiting the orbiting electrons to a series of circular orbits having discrete radii, he proposed that radiated from hydrogen atoms only when an electron made a transition from an outer orbit to one closer to the nucleus. The energy lost by the electron in the abrupt transition is precisely the same as the energy of the quantum emitted light .
