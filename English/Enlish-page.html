            Understanding subject and verb agreement with intervening phrases.
            
            
            1. What is the correct way to write this sentence? The girl climbed the tree, with blue hair.
            
            “With blue hair, the girl climbed the tree.”
            “Climbed the tree, the girl with blue hair.”
            “The girl, with blue hair, climbed the tree.”
            “Leave as is.”
            
            
            
            2. What is the correct way to write this sentence? The dog, with one blue eye, is mine.
            
            “The dog is mine with one blue eye.”
            “With one blue eye the dog is mine.”
            “Leave as is.”
